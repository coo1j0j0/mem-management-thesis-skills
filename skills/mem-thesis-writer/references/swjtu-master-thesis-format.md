# SWJTU Master Thesis Format Reference

Source document: extracted from the user's local file `0开题材料/扉页及范式—硕士.docx`.

Use this reference when the user asks for 西南交通大学硕士论文、MEM论文、工程管理专业硕士论文的封面、扉页、格式、目录、摘要、参考文献、印制顺序或提交前格式审查。This is a hard-format reference, not a general writing prompt. If the school publishes a newer template, the newer official file wins.

## Authority Boundary

- Treat this file as a school template breakdown for SWJTU master's thesis formatting.
- Preserve the original `.docx` template for layout-heavy pages such as cover, Chinese/English title pages, originality statement, authorization page, and signature/date areas when the source file is available.
- Do not rewrite fixed declaration or authorization wording unless the user provides a newer official template.
- Do not invent administrative fields, advisor names, signatures, dates, secrecy level, committee information, or school-specific conclusions. Mark missing fields as `待按学校模板补充`.
- Keep this format reference separate from MEM case-analysis logic. Use it to check form, order, and presentation; use `SKILL.md` for topic, evidence, chapter logic, and thesis prose.

## Master Thesis Front Matter

Required front matter order in the template:

1. Chinese cover.
2. Chinese title page / 扉页.
3. English title page / English thesis information page.
4. 西南交通大学学位论文独创性声明.
5. 西南交通大学学位论文使用授权.
6. 中文摘要.
7. 英文摘要.
8. 目录.
9. 图目录 / 表目录, only when needed.
10. 主要符号表 / 缩略词表, only when needed.

### Cover And Title Page

- Chinese cover fields include 中国图书分类号、密级、国际图书分类号、单位代码、论文题目、学科专业、学号、作者姓名、指导老师、学院、日期.
- Non-secret or internal theses use `公开`; secret theses use the format `密级★保密期限`.
- Chinese thesis title should be concise and normally should not exceed 25 Chinese characters. Avoid uncommon abbreviations, initials, symbols, codes, and formulas.
- If a title is too layered, use a main title plus subtitle. Chinese main/subtitle use a dash; English main/subtitle use a colon. Keep the title identical everywhere in the thesis.
- `指导教师` follows the registered responsible advisor. The cover uses one advisor. The title page may include up to two advisors if joint supervision is officially registered, separated with `、`.
- `学科专业` should follow the graduate management system and approved degree-discipline directory.
- For MEM, the appendix maps `工程管理硕士（MEM）` to `工程管理硕士专业学位`.

### Originality And Authorization Pages

- Signature and date fields must be complete unless the submission is a blind-review version.
- For paper copies, the student and advisor sign in the required places.
- For electronic submission, originality and authorization pages should be scanned pages containing the required signatures.
- Keep the official declaration and authorization text unchanged unless an updated official template is supplied.

## Abstract And Keywords

- Chinese abstract should be an independent, self-contained summary. It should cover research purpose, content, method, results, and conclusion, with emphasis on results and conclusion.
- It should be complete, objective, accurate, and not inflated. Avoid excessive background, broad significance claims, concept explanation, and common-sense theory.
- Normally use third-person narrative, such as `分析了……原因`, `研究了……`, `对……进行了探讨`, `给出了……结论`. First person is only acceptable when expressing the author's own judgment or experience.
- Avoid figures, tables, chemical structures, non-common symbols, uncommon terms, and literature citation numbers in the abstract.
- Chinese master's abstract is normally no more than 800 Chinese characters and should fit within one page.
- English abstract starts on a new page. The title is `ABSTRACT`, and the content should correspond to the Chinese abstract. For a master's thesis, translate 学位论文 as `Thesis`.
- Keywords are usually 3 to 8 words or terms. They must reflect the thesis topic and have clear appearances in the thesis. Avoid overly broad words such as `方法`, `理论`, or `分析`.
- Chinese keywords are written after one blank line from the abstract text, aligned at the left, and separated by Chinese commas. If keywords wrap to another line, use hanging indentation.
- English keywords should correspond to Chinese keywords, with each word capitalized.

## Directory And Optional Lists

- The table of contents starts from `第1章` and continues through the last page.
- Include body headings down to chapter, section, and subsection levels, with starting page numbers.
- Figure and table lists are optional. Use them only when the thesis contains many figures or tables.
- Symbols, acronyms, units, and custom terms can be collected into notation/acronym tables when numerous. If only a few appear, omit the table and explain them at first use.
- Acronym tables should be sorted by English abbreviation.

## Body And End Matter

The thesis body normally contains:

1. 绪论.
2. 具体研究内容.
3. 结论.
4. 致谢.
5. 参考文献.

End matter normally contains:

1. 附录, optional.
2. 攻读学位期间取得的研究成果.

### Introduction

The introduction should explain topic background and significance, domestic and international research progress and review, research gaps, the problem to solve, main theories and methods, research logic, and thesis structure. Literature cited in the introduction must be accurately marked.

### Main Chapters

Main chapters should stay factual, complete, logical, focused, and concise. The chapters should revolve around one theme and form a connected whole. Data and reasoning must be reliable. Do not mix other people's theories or results with the author's own analysis without citation.

Each chapter should normally end with `本章小结`. Method/material chapters may omit it when unsuitable.

### Conclusion

The conclusion is not a simple repetition of chapter summaries. It should state the final view, creative work or contribution, limitations, and future work or suggestions.

### Acknowledgements

Acknowledgements are normally no more than 800 Chinese characters. Thank only real funders, organizations, individuals, advice, help, permissions, materials, images, literature, research ideas, or design owners.

## Formatting Rules

### Language And Expression

- Chinese theses should use standard Chinese characters.
- Technical terms, symbols, quantities, and units should follow relevant national standards.
- Figure titles, table titles, axes, legends, and headers should use Chinese, except for accepted technical symbols.
- Avoid literary, emotional, promotional, or non-academic wording.
- The thesis author is singular; avoid using `我们`.

### Heading Levels

- Headings should be focused, concise, and normally fit on one line.
- Do not add punctuation to headings.
- Avoid English abbreviations in headings unless they are standard in the field.
- Use clear hierarchy. Normally stop at level 3, and do not exceed level 4.
- All levels should have titles. Use one Chinese-character space between heading number and title.
- Chapter headings are centered. Lower-level headings are left aligned.

### Fonts And Paragraphs

- Unless otherwise specified, Chinese text uses 宋体. English letters and numbers use Times New Roman.
- From the Chinese abstract onward, paragraphs and headings use fixed 20 pt line spacing.
- Body paragraphs are justified and first-line indented by 2 Chinese characters.
- When Chinese is 黑体 or bold, corresponding English and numbers should use bold Times New Roman.

### Figures, Tables, Equations, Units, Numbers, And Footnotes

- Tables normally use three-line style.
- Figure/table notes are written separately under the figure or table when needed.
- Figures and tables should not be inserted without explanation in the body text.
- Equation numbers are right-aligned and do not use leader dots.
- Follow GB 3100, GB/T 3101, and related standards for quantities and units.
- Use one character space between non-Chinese numbers and units, such as `1 m`.
- Follow the national standards for punctuation and numbers. Use Arabic numerals except where Chinese numerals are conventional.
- Footnotes explain or supplement specific content. They should appear at the bottom of the page, not as inline parenthetical explanation. Number footnotes per page from `①`, with small-five font, single spacing, justified text, and hanging indent.

### Page Setup, Header, And Page Numbers

- Paper size: A4 white 70-80 g/m2 paper, except color illustration pages when needed.
- Margins: top 3.0 cm, bottom 3.0 cm, left 3.0 cm, right 3.0 cm, binding gutter 0 cm.
- Header from edge: 2 cm. Footer from edge: 2 cm.
- No headers before the Chinese abstract, including cover, title pages, originality statement, and authorization page.
- From Chinese abstract onward, headers are required.
- Front-matter headers use the current part title, such as `摘要`, `ABSTRACT`, `目录`, `图目录`, `表目录`, `主要符号表`, or `缩略词表`.
- From the first page of Chapter 1 to the end, the header is `西南交通大学博/硕士学位论文`.
- Header uses centered five-size text, Chinese 宋体 and English/numbers Times New Roman, with a single 0.75 pt header line.
- Page numbers are centered at the bottom, small-five Times New Roman, without decorative dashes.
- Chinese abstract through optional notation/acronym lists use uppercase Roman numerals `Ⅰ、Ⅱ、Ⅲ……`, starting from `Ⅰ`.
- From `第1章` onward, use Arabic numerals `1, 2, 3...`, starting from `1`.

## References

- References appear after the body and start on a new page.
- Every cited source must be listed; uncited sources must not be listed.
- Use sequential numerical citations in the order of first appearance, with square brackets.
- Repeated citations use the original number from the first citation.
- Do not place citation numbers in section headings, except citation in figure/table titles where applicable.
- For multiple references in one place, list all numbers in square brackets and connect continuous numbers with a hyphen.
- As a sentence element, citation numbers are not superscripted, e.g. `由文献[4, 7-10]可知`.
- Reference formatting follows GB/T 7714-2015.
- Professional master's theses generally need at least 30 references. Foreign-language references should generally be at least half of the total. References from the last five years should generally be at least one third, and there should be recent two-year references plus some theses or professional monographs.
- Online sources and standards are not counted toward the above reference quantity requirements.
- Product manuals, unpublished research reports, and inaccessible materials normally should not be used as references, except well-known internal reports or major company technical reports where appropriate.
- The author's own degree-period publications should be listed under `攻读学位期间取得的研究成果`, not ordinary references.

## Printing And Binding

Binding order:

1. Thesis cover.
2. Chinese and English title pages.
3. Originality statement.
4. Thesis authorization.
5. Chinese abstract.
6. English abstract.
7. Table of contents.
8. Figure/table lists, if needed.
9. Acronym or notation lists, if needed.
10. Body: introduction, main chapters, conclusion, acknowledgements, references.
11. Appendices.
12. Degree-period research achievements.

Printing rules:

- Before Chinese abstract, front matter is single-sided.
- Chinese abstract and later front matter are double-sided. If a part has an odd number of pages, the back side is blank.
- From Chapter 1 to the end, use double-sided printing.
- Each chapter's first page should be on an odd-numbered page.
- For non-blind-review submission, cover and title page information must be complete.

## MEM Thesis Checkpoints

When applying this reference to a MEM thesis, check these points:

- Degree category: `工程管理硕士（MEM）` corresponds to `工程管理硕士专业学位`.
- The title should remain management-problem oriented and concise. Do not add method names or frameworks to the title unless the school/advisor requires it.
- Abstract should state management research purpose, case/project object, methods, diagnosis or findings, optimization path, and bounded conclusion. Do not claim strong measured effects without source evidence.
- Keywords should be concrete topic terms from the thesis, e.g. project/process/EMR/MEM-specific terms, not generic words such as `管理`, `方法`, or `分析`.
- References should meet professional master's expectations: at least 30, with foreign-language, recent, degree-thesis, and monograph support where available. Do not count standards and web sources toward the core number.
- If the thesis uses few figures or tables, omit figure/table lists unless the school or advisor requires them.
- Keep declarations, authorization, signature/date pages as template-driven pages, not prose-generation tasks.
